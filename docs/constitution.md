# Constitution

This constitution defines the non-negotiable rules of Converge.

## Article I: Purpose

Converge exists to improve technical decisions before implementation.

## Article II: Scope

Converge governs analysis, review, deliberation, approval, rejection, and reflection. It does not prescribe code generation techniques.

## Article III: Canonical Artifacts

The canonical artifacts are the decision cycle, council model, engines, gates, critique system, templates, and specification.

## Article IV: Integrity

Every recommendation must show how it was derived from evidence, criteria, and constraints. Hidden heuristics are not acceptable.

## Article V: Accountability

Every approval and rejection must identify the criteria used and the evidence that supported the outcome.

## Article VI: Portability

Converge must remain independent of any single AI tool, vendor, language, runtime, or workflow engine.

## Article VII: Extensibility

New domain packs may extend the framework, but they may not weaken the core decision model or redefine the meaning of canonical outputs.
