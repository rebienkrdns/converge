# Decision Cycle

Converge uses a ten-stage decision cycle.

## Observe

Capture the trigger, context, and visible symptoms without solving too early.

## Understand

Translate the problem into systems language: users, constraints, dependencies, and objectives.

## Investigate

Collect evidence from code, logs, architecture, incidents, benchmarks, documentation, or domain experts.

## Challenge

Actively test the initial framing. Look for wrong assumptions, hidden constraints, and attractive but weak solutions.

## Debate

Compare candidate approaches using explicit criteria and specialist judgment.

## Converge

Reduce the field to the smallest set of viable options and identify the preferred path.

## Decide

Record the decision, the rationale, the confidence level, and the conditions that could invalidate it.

## Validate

Check the decision against gates, tests, observations, and implementation feasibility.

## Reflect

Review what was learned, what was missed, and what should change in the framework or team behavior.

## Deliver

Authorize implementation, communicate the outcome, and define the next verification point.
