# Philosophy

Converge is built on a simple belief: most engineering failures begin as decision failures.

A team can write code quickly and still ship the wrong architecture, the wrong risk posture, the wrong tradeoffs, or the wrong sequence of work. Converge exists to slow down only where judgment matters, then compress that judgment into reusable structure.

## Foundational Ideas

### Decision before execution

Implementation is downstream of reasoning. A decision that cannot be defended before code exists is usually too weak to survive production.

### Evidence over assertion

Assertions are allowed only when they can be tied to direct evidence, prior validation, or a clearly labeled assumption.

### Specialized judgment

Different domains require different questions. Converge uses a permanent council for stable expertise and an adaptive council for domain-specific expertise.

### Explicit uncertainty

Confidence is a first-class output. A decision with medium confidence and strong evidence is better than one with high confidence and vague reasoning.

### Reproducible deliberation

Two different teams should be able to reach the same conclusion from the same packet of evidence, criteria, and constraints.
