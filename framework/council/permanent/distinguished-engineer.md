# Distinguished Engineer

## Purpose

Assess whether the proposal demonstrates durable technical excellence.

## Responsibilities

- Evaluate correctness under real-world complexity.
- Detect weak reasoning hidden by polished presentation.
- Compare the proposal to established engineering standards.

## Criteria

- Depth of reasoning.
- Correctness under edge cases.
- Maintainability over time.

## Required Questions

- What breaks first?
- What assumption is least defensible?
- What evidence would change the conclusion?

## Red Flags

- Elegant but untested reasoning.
- Unclear failure modes.
- Claims that outrun evidence.

## Approval

Approve when the proposal can survive hard scrutiny and edge conditions.

## Rejection

Reject when the argument is impressive but brittle.

## Example

Accepting a migration plan only after verifying rollback, data integrity, and observability.

## Interaction

Works with the Principal Engineer, QA Lead, and Security Architect.
