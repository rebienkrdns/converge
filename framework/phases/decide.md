# Decide

## Purpose

Commit the chosen direction into a durable, traceable decision record. A decision that is not recorded did not happen. The decision record is the primary artifact of the Converge cycle and the primary input to every future change that touches the same area.

## Entry Condition

The Converge phase has produced a single preferred direction with complete rationale and internal consistency validation.

## Process

1. State the decision clearly and completely. The statement must be specific enough that someone reading it six months later, without context, would understand exactly what was decided.
2. State the confidence level and the basis for it. Confidence is derived from evidence quality, assumption weight, and the degree of specialist disagreement. High confidence requires strong evidence, low-weight assumptions, and minimal unresolved disagreement.
3. Record the alternatives that were considered and the reason each was not chosen.
4. State the reversal conditions: the specific circumstances, evidence, or events that would require this decision to be revisited. A decision without reversal conditions is a decision that cannot be maintained.
5. Record the active assumptions: the claims that the decision depends on that are not yet fully evidenced. Each assumption should be paired with the monitoring or validation step that would confirm or invalidate it.
6. Record any dissenting positions with their reasoning.
7. Assign the decision to an owner who is accountable for its execution and its revision if reversal conditions are met.

## Outputs

- Decision statement.
- Confidence level (Low / Medium / High) with explicit basis.
- Alternatives considered and rejection rationale for each.
- Reversal conditions.
- Active assumption list with associated monitoring plan.
- Recorded dissent.
- Decision owner and date.

## Exit Condition

The decision record is complete, signed, and stored in a location accessible to every stakeholder who will be affected by it. The decision owner is identified and has accepted responsibility.

## Failure Signals

- The decision statement is vague enough that two people could interpret it differently.
- Confidence is stated without basis ("High confidence because the team agrees").
- No reversal conditions are defined.
- Alternatives are not recorded because the team "already knew" what they were.
- The decision owner is a committee rather than a named individual.

## Interaction with Subsequent Phases

The decision record is the input to Validate. Gates check the decision against structural requirements. If a gate blocks, the decision record must be updated, not abandoned. Reflect uses the decision record as the reference point for evaluating the actual outcome.
