# Reflect

## Purpose

Review the outcome of the decision cycle against what was expected, and extract learnings that improve future decisions. Reflection is not a retrospective on feelings. It is a systematic comparison between what the cycle predicted and what the system actually produced, used to calibrate the framework and the council.

## Entry Condition

Enough time has passed after delivery to observe meaningful outcomes. Reversal conditions and success criteria from Decide have been monitored. This phase may be triggered immediately after delivery, or after a defined observation window.

## Process

1. Compare the actual outcome to the success criteria defined in Understand. Was each criterion met, partially met, or not met. For each unmet criterion, identify whether the gap was in the decision itself, in the implementation, or in the original criterion definition.
2. Review the reversal conditions. Were any met or approached. If a reversal condition was met and the team did not act, identify why.
3. Evaluate the quality of the evidence used in Investigate. Did the evidence predict the outcome correctly. Which evidence was most valuable. Which evidence was misleading.
4. Evaluate the quality of the assumptions listed in Decide. Which assumptions held. Which were invalidated. What would have changed if the invalidated assumptions had been identified earlier.
5. Identify the earliest phase in the cycle where a different action would have produced a better outcome.
6. Extract one or more specific improvements: to the council composition, to the evidence collection process, to the gate definitions, or to the framework itself.

## Outputs

- Outcome comparison: actual vs. predicted for each success criterion.
- Reversal condition status.
- Evidence quality retrospective.
- Assumption accuracy review.
- Phase where the most leverage existed for a better outcome.
- Specific improvement proposals for the framework or process.

## Exit Condition

At least one concrete, actionable improvement has been identified and assigned to an owner. The improvement is specific enough to be applied to the next decision cycle that enters a similar domain.

## Failure Signals

- Reflect is skipped because delivery was successful.
- Reflect is skipped because delivery failed and the team wants to move on.
- The phase produces feelings-based retrospective rather than outcome-based analysis.
- No improvements are extracted because "we did everything right."
- Improvements are extracted but not assigned to an owner or incorporated into the framework.

## Interaction with Subsequent Phases

Reflect feeds forward into future cycles. Improvements to the framework modify the gates, council composition, or phase definitions. Evidence quality learnings modify the investigation standards. Assumption accuracy learnings modify the confidence calibration. Reflect is the mechanism by which Converge improves over time.
