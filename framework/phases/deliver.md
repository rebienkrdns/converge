# Deliver

## Purpose

Authorize implementation, communicate the decision to all affected parties, and define the conditions under which the delivery will be considered successful. Delivery is not the end of the decision process. It is the transition from decision to execution, and it must be as precise as the decision itself.

## Entry Condition

Validate has cleared all gates or all blocking conditions have been resolved. The decision record is complete and final.

## Process

1. Issue the formal authorization to proceed. The authorization must reference the decision record and name the authorizing parties.
2. Communicate the decision to all stakeholders who will be affected by the implementation: the delivery team, adjacent teams, dependent systems owners, and any external parties who need to prepare.
3. Define the next verification point: a specific date, milestone, or observable signal at which the outcome will be evaluated against the success criteria from Understand.
4. Communicate the reversal conditions to the delivery team. The team must know what to watch for and what action to take if a reversal condition is met during or after implementation.
5. Confirm the monitoring plan: what metrics, logs, or signals will be used to evaluate the outcome, and who is responsible for reviewing them.
6. Transfer any open conditional pass items from Validate to the delivery team with explicit owners and deadlines.

## Outputs

- Formal authorization record referencing the decision.
- Stakeholder communication with the decision summary and implementation timeline.
- Verification point: date or condition, success criteria, and responsible party.
- Reversal condition briefing for the delivery team.
- Monitoring plan with assigned owners.
- Open conditional items from Validate with owners and deadlines.

## Exit Condition

The delivery team is authorized and briefed. All affected stakeholders have been notified. The verification point is scheduled. No ambiguity remains about what success looks like, who is responsible for what, and what happens if a reversal condition is met.

## Failure Signals

- Implementation begins before formal authorization is issued.
- Stakeholders learn about the decision from the implementation rather than from communication.
- No verification point is defined because "we'll check when it's done."
- Reversal conditions were documented in the decision record but not communicated to the delivery team.
- Open conditional items from Validate are handed over without owners or deadlines.

## Interaction with Subsequent Phases

Deliver transitions the cycle from decision to execution. Reflect is triggered at the verification point or when reversal conditions are approached. The quality of the Deliver phase determines whether Reflect has the information it needs to evaluate the outcome accurately.
