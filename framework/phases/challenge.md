# Challenge

## Purpose

Stress-test the problem framing, evidence quality, and any early directional preference before commitment calcifies. This phase is not about creating conflict. It is about ensuring that confidence earned during Investigate is warranted rather than assumed.

## Entry Condition

The evidence record, assumption list, and sensitivity analysis from Investigate are complete.

## Process

1. Re-examine the root question from Understand. Challenge whether it is the right question, whether it is too narrow or too broad, and whether answering it would actually resolve the original trigger.
2. Examine the highest-weight assumptions. For each, ask: what happens to the preferred direction if this assumption is false.
3. Introduce the strongest alternative framing. If the current framing is "this is a performance problem", the challenge might be "this is a data model problem producing performance symptoms."
4. Identify what the council may be missing due to team composition, available tooling, time pressure, or familiarity bias.
5. Validate scope boundaries from Understand. Challenge whether something was excluded that should be in scope, or whether the scope was drawn around a familiar area rather than the actual problem boundary.
6. Apply the Devil's Advocate role explicitly: the weakest argument against the current direction is insufficient. Only the strongest opposing case is useful.

## Outputs

- Challenge record: each tested assumption with the result (confirmed, weakened, or invalidated).
- Alternative framings considered and why they were accepted, modified, or rejected.
- Scope boundary validation: any scope changes agreed upon.
- Identified blind spots and how they will be managed.
- Revised sensitivity analysis if any assumptions changed.

## Exit Condition

The council has actively attempted to break the current framing and evidence base. The preferred direction, if one exists, has survived challenge or has been adjusted as a result of it. No council member has an unvoiced objection at this point.

## Failure Signals

- Challenge is treated as a formality and completed in minutes.
- The Devil's Advocate role is assigned to the person who agrees most with the current direction.
- Alternative framings are dismissed without explicit reasoning.
- The scope never changes between Understand and the end of Challenge, regardless of evidence.
- Objections surface after this phase concludes rather than during it.

## Interaction with Subsequent Phases

Challenge is the last point where the framing itself can change without significant cost. After Challenge, Debate operates on a shared and tested problem definition. A framing weakness discovered in Validate or after delivery is far more expensive than one discovered here.
